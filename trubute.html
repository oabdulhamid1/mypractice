Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

January 15, 1929 - April 4, 1968

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. graduated from Booker T. Washington High School entering Morehouse College at the young age of 15. This soon to be Leader of the Civil Rights movement continued his education at Morehouse with a major in Sociology and graduated from Morehouse in 1948 with a Bachelor Degree of Arts. This great man continued his education from 1948 - 1951 at Cozer Theological Seminary thus allowing him to obtain a Bachelor of Divinity Degree. In 1995, he became one of the few black men who had received a PhD by graduating from Boston University. For all his hard work he was awarded a Doctorate of Philosophy in Systematic Theology. His educational history created a great leader for his fellow black brethren.

He strongly believed love and Christianity would be the best avenue for change. During the Civil Rights Movement he became the voice of many.

Thoughout his career he emulated the message that blacks must believe that they are not less than anyone else and that society's treatment of the African American community is unjust and morally wrong. He believed that non-violent civil disobedience would lead to great change. He often pointed out unjust laws by illustrating these laws were in conflict with moral laws taught to every American.

Additionally, he warned his supporters that one must be willing to suffer the consequences of civil disobedience. He believed the consequences inflicted on his supporters would lead to a change in societiesâ€™ moral compass.

Martin Luther King Jr. believed he could change social awareness leading the vast majority of Americans to reject their racist ideology blindly practiced for so long.

He was thrust into the spot light of the Civil Rights Movement with the successful execution of the Montgomery bus boycott which ended segregation on buses. He participated in several sit-in demonstration with students to end the segregation of lunch counters in the South. And he was a key speaker at the March on Washington where he delivered what is considered to be one of the most prolific speeches in this country: "I have a dream". He participated in the Salma march to ensure voting rights for blacks were enforced.

Consequentially, the Civil rights act was passed on July 2, 1964 and the fair voting act was passed on August 6, 1965.

Martin Luther King Jr's. character is depicted in his quote in a Morehouse College student paper The Maroon Tiger: "We must remember that intelligence is not enough. Intelligence plus character - that is the goal of true education".

On October 14, 1964 Martin Luther King received the Nobel peace prize for his non-violent tactics and gave a humble acceptance speech thanking 100 of thousands of less known participants of the movement without whom the movement would not have been successful.

Martin Luther King continued to advocate for social justice by addressing the issues of poverty, fair housing and the ending of the Vietnam war.

However, his life ended suddenly at the Lorraine motel in Memphis Tennessee on April 4, 1968 where he was assassinated. He was there to support fair working conditions and pay for the city sanitation workers.

The effect of his death was felt throughout the country as people mourned their leader and inspiration.

His wife Coletta King founded The King Center and advocated to have a national holiday in honor of Dr. Martin Luther King.

On November 2, 1983 Presindent Ronald Regan designated a national holiday on the 3rd Monday of every January to celebrate Martin Luther King's birthday.
